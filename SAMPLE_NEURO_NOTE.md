He’s also complaining of some mild cognitive slowing, or "brain fog" as he calls it, and significant fatigue that doesn't improve with rest. Given the constellation of symptoms, we need to rule out a demyelinating process. Let's order an MRI of the brain and the entire spine, with and without gadolinium, looking for MS-type plaques. I also want a lumbar puncture to check for oligoclonal bands and elevated IgG index. Let's schedule him for visual evoked potentials too, just to see if there's subclinical optic nerve involvement. Start him on a short course of oral prednisone if symptoms acutely worsen before the imaging, but let's get that MRI done stat. I’ll see him back in two weeks to review the scans."
