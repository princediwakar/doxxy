Patient is a 45-year-old male presenting with a two-week history of persistent lower back pain, radiating down the right leg. He reports the pain worsens with prolonged sitting and is only slightly alleviated by over-the-counter NSAIDs. No recent history of trauma or heavy lifting. On physical examination, the straight leg raise test is positive on the right side at 45 degrees. Sensation is intact bilaterally. Reflexes are two plus and symmetric. Assessment: Suspected lumbar radiculopathy, likely L5-S1. Plan: Order an MRI of the lumbar spine, refer to physical therapy for an initial evaluation, and schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks.
