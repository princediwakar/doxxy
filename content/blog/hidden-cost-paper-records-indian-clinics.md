## What Paper Actually Costs Your Clinic

The obvious costs come first: folders, cabinets, printing, and the square footage to store them. A mid-size clinic with 2,000 active patients typically spends INR 15,000–25,000 a year on physical record supplies and storage space alone.

The real cost is clinical. When a patient walks in after 8 months, the paper file is either missing, illegible, or buried under a stack of newer cases. You're making treatment decisions with incomplete history — because reconstructing the timeline from scattered pages takes longer than the consultation itself.

Then there's the human cost. Staff spend hours filing, retrieving, and re-filing. A front-desk coordinator making INR 20,000 a month who spends 40% of her day managing paper records costs you INR 8,000 a month — for work that disappears with digital records.
