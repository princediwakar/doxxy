A dental clinic in Pune ran out of lidocaine on a Tuesday morning. Three root canals were scheduled. The nearest pharmacy that stocked dental-grade anesthetic was 40 minutes away. Two patients left. One waited an hour in the chair.

Inventory mistakes don't just cost you the sale of a medicine. They cost you the procedure that depended on it — and the patient's trust that your clinic has its act together.

## Why Clinic Inventory Is Harder Than It Looks

Pharmacy inventory sits at an awkward intersection. It's not retail — you're not a chemist shop optimizing for walk-in sales. It's not pure hospital inventory either — you don't have a dedicated procurement department and a six-figure inventory management system.

Most clinics run on guesswork. The senior nurse checks the cupboard on Friday. Someone notices the paracetamol strips are low. Someone else orders more — but can't remember whether the last batch was from the manufacturer that charges INR 42 or the one that charges INR 58. Receipts pile up. Expiry dates pass unnoticed until a patient points out that the sample pack says 2024.
