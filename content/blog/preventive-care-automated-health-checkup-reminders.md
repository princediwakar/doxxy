A multi-specialty clinic in Nagpur ran the numbers on their health checkup packages. They had great packages — comprehensive, reasonably priced, covering the screenings their patient demographic actually needed. But uptake was low. Only 8% of their active patients had booked a checkup in the past year.

The problem wasn't the packages. It was that nobody reminded patients they were due for one.

They set up automated recall reminders through Doxxy — patients received an SMS when they were due for an annual checkup, with a link to book directly. Within four months, checkup bookings tripled. The revenue from the additional checkups alone covered two years of their entire practice management platform.

## The Recall Gap

Every clinic knows which patients are due for checkups, vaccinations, and follow-up screenings. But knowing and acting are different things. Without an automated system, recalls happen manually — a staff member has to pull a list, make phone calls, and track responses. In a busy clinic, that work gets pushed to "next week" indefinitely.

The result: patients fall out of care. Diabetic patients skip their HbA1c. Women miss their annual screenings. Children fall behind on vaccinations. The clinical consequences are serious. The revenue consequences are significant.

## What an Automated Recall System Does

Doxxy's recall system works on rules you set once:

**Annual health checkups.** Every adult patient gets a reminder 12 months after their last checkup. They see available time slots and book instantly.

**Chronic disease monitoring.** Diabetic patients get reminders for quarterly HbA1c. Hypertensive patients get reminded for blood pressure checks. Thyroid patients get reminded for TSH panels. The interval is configurable per condition.

**Vaccination schedules.** Pediatric patients get reminders aligned with the national immunization schedule. Adult patients get flu shot reminders before monsoon season. Travel vaccine reminders go out based on patient history.

**Preventive screenings.** Women over 40 get mammogram reminders. Patients over 50 get colonoscopy reminders. The system tracks age and risk factors automatically.

## The Message That Works

The content of the reminder matters. "You're due for a checkup" gets ignored. "Your last health checkup was February 2025. Book your annual screening this week — here are available slots" gets booked.

Specificity drives action. Include the date of their last visit. Include a direct booking link — one tap. Include a brief note on why the checkup matters for their specific profile.

## The Clinical and Financial Case

Preventive care is better medicine. Catching a condition early means better outcomes, simpler treatment, and lower costs for the patient. It also builds long-term patient relationships — the patient who comes in annually for a checkup is your patient for life.

The financial case is straightforward. A basic health checkup package at INR 1,500, booked by 100 additional patients a year, generates INR 1,50,000 in direct revenue — plus the follow-up consultations and treatments that result from abnormal findings.

For a clinic already running at capacity on acute care, preventive checkups fill quieter weekday morning slots that would otherwise go empty.
