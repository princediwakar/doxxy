A cardiologist in Delhi received roughly 15 patient referrals a month from local general physicians. His referral tracking system was a WhatsApp group and a notebook. When asked how many referred patients actually booked an appointment, he didn't know. When asked which GPs sent the most referrals, he guessed. When asked how many referred patients he sent back to the referring doctor with a treatment summary, he admitted it rarely happened.

He was leaving revenue on the table and straining referral relationships — not because he was a bad doctor, but because he had no system.

Referrals are the lifeblood of specialty practices. A structured referral management system turns an unreliable word-of-mouth channel into a predictable patient acquisition engine.

## Where Referrals Get Lost

The typical referral journey in an Indian clinic has at least four failure points:

**The scrap of paper.** A GP writes a specialist's name on a prescription pad. The patient folds it, puts it in their pocket, and forgets about it. The referral dies at the point of creation.

**The phone call that isn't answered.** The patient calls the specialist's clinic during peak hours. The line is busy. They try once more, then give up. The referral dies at the scheduling step.

**The missing feedback loop.** The patient sees the specialist, who never informs the GP about the consultation outcome. The GP feels ignored. Next time, they refer to someone else. The referral relationship dies silently.

**The untracked outcome.** The specialist has no idea how many referrals converted, which sources are growing, or which are drying up. Decisions about where to invest relationship-building effort are made on gut feeling.

## What a Digital Referral System Looks Like

Doxxy's referral management turns this unreliable chain into a tracked, automated workflow:

**Structured referral creation.** The referring doctor creates a digital referral with the patient's details, reason for referral, relevant history, and preferred specialist. The patient receives an SMS with the specialist's booking link. No paper. No lost phone numbers.

**Conversion tracking.** Every referral is tracked from creation to appointment completion. The specialist knows exactly how many referrals came from each source, how many converted, and the revenue generated. The referring doctor can see that their patient was seen.

**Closed-loop reporting.** After the consultation, the specialist sends a structured summary back to the referring doctor through the platform. The GP knows what happened, what treatment was recommended, and what follow-up is needed. The professional relationship strengthens with every closed loop.

**Referral analytics.** Which referral sources are growing? Which are declining? What's the average time from referral to appointment? Which specialties receive the most referrals? Data replaces guesswork.

## For Referring Doctors: Keep Your Patients in the Loop

GPs who refer patients want to know two things: did my patient get seen, and what happened? When they get neither, they find a specialist who communicates better.

Doxxy lets referring doctors track the status of every referral they send. They see when the appointment is booked, when it's completed, and they receive the specialist's summary. The patient feels cared for. The GP feels respected. The specialist earns a reliable referral source.

## For Specialists: Know Where Your Patients Come From

A specialist who tracks referrals discovers patterns they never noticed. One source sends high-value surgical cases. Another sends routine consultations. One GP's referrals always book within two days. Another's referrals have a 40% no-show rate.

This data tells you where to invest your relationship-building time. Send a thank-you note to the GP who sent 12 referrals last quarter. Offer a CME talk to the clinic whose referrals convert at the highest rate. Check in with the source whose referrals have declined — there might be a fixable reason.
