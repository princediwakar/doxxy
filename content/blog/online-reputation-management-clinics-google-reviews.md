A patient in Chennai searches "ENT specialist near me" on Google. Three clinics appear in the local pack. One has 4.8 stars and 147 reviews with photos of a clean, modern facility. Another has 3.9 stars and 12 reviews, the most recent from eight months ago. The third has no reviews at all.

The patient calls the first one.

This happens thousands of times a day across India. And if your clinic isn't actively managing its online reputation, you're invisible to the patients who are looking for you right now.

## The Numbers That Matter

73% of patients in urban India check Google reviews before choosing a healthcare provider. 68% won't consider a clinic with a rating below 4.0 stars. And 81% read reviews from the past three months — because they want to know what the experience is like now, not three years ago.

Yet most clinic owners don't know what their Google rating is. They've never responded to a review — positive or negative. They haven't claimed their Google Business Profile. Their online presence is whatever patients decided to post, with no input from the clinic.

## Claim Your Profile First

Before anything else, search for your clinic on Google. If a business profile exists but you haven't claimed it, claim it. Fill out every field: address, phone number, hours, services, photos. A complete profile ranks higher in local search and converts more viewers into patients.

Add real photos of your clinic — reception area, consultation rooms, equipment. Stock photos feel inauthentic. Patients want to see where they're going.

## Ask for Reviews (Systematically)

Most clinics that have few reviews don't have them because patients are unhappy — they have few reviews because nobody asked. Satisfied patients rarely think to leave a review unprompted.

The best time to ask is right after a positive interaction. Doxxy's post-consultation workflow can include an automated SMS with a direct link to your Google review page. The message goes out while the experience is fresh. Clinics using automated review requests typically see 3–5x more reviews within the first month.

Make it easy. Include the direct review link. One tap should take them there.

## Respond to Every Review

A response to a positive review shows you're paying attention. A thoughtful response to a negative review shows you care about fixing problems. Both signal to prospective patients that you're engaged and professional.

Negative reviews aren't the enemy. An ignored negative review is the enemy. A 3-star review with a genuine, empathetic response from the doctor builds more trust than a stack of unreplied 5-star reviews.

Keep responses professional. Thank the patient for their feedback. Address the specific concern without being defensive. Offer to take the conversation offline. Never share patient information in a public response.

## The Local SEO Bonus

Google's local ranking algorithm weighs three factors heavily: relevance, distance, and prominence. Reviews feed directly into prominence. A clinic with more reviews, higher ratings, and recent review activity will outrank a competitor with a stale profile — even if they're physically closer to the searcher.

A complete Google Business Profile with active review management is the highest-ROI marketing activity a clinic can do. It's free. It compounds over time. And it directly drives new patient acquisition.
