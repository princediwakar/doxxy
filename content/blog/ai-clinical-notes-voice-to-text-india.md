She now dictates those notes. The system structures them. She leaves on time.

## The Real Problem With Manual Clinical Notes

It's not just about time. When you type during a consultation, you break eye contact. You lose the non-verbal cues that often carry the most important clinical information. The patient feels unheard. You feel like a data entry clerk.

Dictating after the fact isn't much better. Memory decays. Details blur. The note you write at 8 PM about a 2 PM patient is worse than the note you could have dictated at 2:05 PM.

Doxxy's voice-to-text engine — built on Sarvam AI's speech recognition — addresses both problems. Dictate during or immediately after the consultation, and the system handles the rest.

## From Voice to SOAP Format, Automatically

The workflow is simple. After a consultation, the clinician dictates their notes in natural language — exactly as they'd describe the case to a colleague. Doxxy's AI structures the raw dictation into proper SOAP format:

**Subjective** — the patient's reported symptoms and history
**Objective** — examination findings and vitals
**Assessment** — diagnosis and clinical reasoning
**Plan** — treatment, prescriptions, and follow-up instructions

The clinician reviews the structured note, makes any adjustments, and saves it. No typing. No formatting. No copy-pasting between systems.

## Built for India's Linguistic Reality

A clinic in Chennai might see patients who speak Tamil, English, Hindi, and Telugu in a single morning. Doxxy's voice recognition supports 12+ languages including Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, Kannada, and Marathi.

A doctor can dictate in one language while the system outputs the structured clinical note in English — the standard for medical documentation in India. Patients get instructions in their preferred language. The clinical record stays in English for continuity and medico-legal purposes.
