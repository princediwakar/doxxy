## The Manual Billing Tax

Manual invoicing doesn't just waste time. It introduces errors. A mistyped amount. A missed line item. A consultation billed at the wrong rate because the fee schedule changed last month and nobody updated the template.

These errors compound. An undercharge of INR 50 per patient across 600 patients a month is INR 30,000 in lost revenue. An overcharge damages trust and creates an awkward correction conversation.

Then there's follow-up. When a patient doesn't pay at the time of service, someone has to remember. Which means someone has to maintain a separate tracking system — usually a notebook or a spreadsheet — and cross-reference it against the invoices. This is a full-time job disguised as a side task.

## Invoices That Generate Themselves

Doxxy pulls consultation data — patient name, visit type, provider, duration, tests, medications dispensed — and generates a professional, itemized invoice automatically. No typing. No templates. No calculators.

Your records are consistent because every invoice follows the same format with the same data sources. The fee schedule is configured once in the system, so rate changes apply instantly to every new invoice.

## Payment Tracking Without the Spreadsheet

Every invoice in Doxxy carries a visual payment status: paid, pending, or overdue. The dashboard shows you exactly how much is outstanding, from whom, and for how long. No separate ledger. No end-of-month surprise when you realize a dozen patients from three weeks ago still haven't paid.

For clinics that handle insurance, the system tracks claims, approvals, and reimbursements in the same view. You know which claims are pending with which insurer, which are approved, and which need follow-up.
