## Prerequisites

- You must be logged in with a **Staff** or **Superadmin** role in the active clinic.
- The patient must have a consultation (completed or in progress).
- A clinic must be selected.

## The Goal

Generate a professional invoice for a patient's visit — add billable service items (consultation fee, procedures, medicines), apply discounts and taxes, generate a formatted invoice with an invoice number, and mark the payment as received. The invoice can be printed for the patient and is recorded in the clinic's financial records.

## Step-by-Step UI Instructions

### Opening the Billing Modal

1. From the **Schedule page**, select a patient from the queue. The encounter canvas opens in the right panel.

2. Click the **"Bill"** button on the Patient Header (visible in the staff variant with direct action buttons: Bill, Schedule, Edit).

3. Alternatively, from the doctor's three-dot menu on the Patient Header, select **"Bill"**.

4. The **Billing Modal** opens. It is pre-populated with the patient and consultation context.

### Adding Service Items

5. In the **Service Items** section, add each billable item:

   - **Description:** The name of the service or item (e.g., "Consultation - General Medicine", "Blood Test - CBC", "Paracetamol 500mg").
   - **Quantity:** The number of units (default 1).
   - **Unit Price (₹):** The price per unit.
   - **Total:** Auto-calculated as Quantity × Unit Price.

6. Click **"+ Add Item"** to add more line items. Use the X button on any row to remove it.

### Applying Discounts and Taxes

7. Add a **Discount** — either as a percentage or a fixed rupee amount. The discount is subtracted from the subtotal.

8. Add **Tax** if applicable (e.g., GST). Enter the tax percentage. The tax amount is calculated on the discounted subtotal.

### Reviewing the Invoice

9. The modal shows a running calculation:
   - **Subtotal:** Sum of all service items.
   - **Discount:** Amount subtracted.
   - **Tax:** Amount added.
   - **Grand Total:** Final amount payable.

10. Review all line items, discount, and tax before saving.

### Generating the Invoice

11. Click the **save/generate button** (typically "Create Bill" or "Generate Invoice").

12. The invoice is created with:
    - A unique **invoice number** (auto-generated, e.g., "INV-a1b2c3d4").
    - The current date and time.
    - The patient's name and details.
    - The clinic's name, address, and contact info.
    - All service items, discount, tax, and grand total.

13. A green toast confirms: *"Bill created successfully"* (or similar).

### Processing Payment

14. After the invoice is generated, mark the payment as received. The billing flow may include a payment collection step where you record:
    - Payment method (Cash, Card, UPI, etc.).
    - Amount received (full or partial payment).

15. Once payment is marked, the bill is recorded in the financials and the patient's bill history.

### Printing the Invoice

16. From the bill view, use the print function. The `billingPrintUtils` module formats the invoice for clean, professional printing — suitable for handing to the patient or filing.

17. Use Cmd+P / Ctrl+P to print, or the print button if available in the bill detail view.

### Viewing Past Bills

18. In the encounter canvas, expand the **"Bills (N)"** accordion in the Administrative Footer to see all bills for this patient.

19. Click any bill row to view its full details.

## Recommended Clinic Policy & Edge Cases

### Standard Billing Workflow
1. Doctor completes the consultation.
2. Front desk opens the Billing Modal from the Patient Header.
3. Add service items: consultation fee (as per the doctor's profile), any procedures, medicines dispensed.
4. Apply any applicable discounts (e.g., senior citizen discount, staff discount).
5. Apply tax if GST is applicable.
6. Generate the invoice and print a copy for the patient.
7. Collect payment and mark it as received.

### Consultation Fee Alignment
- The doctor's consultation fee (set in their profile) should be used as the consultation line item price. Staff should verify the fee rather than manually entering it from memory.
- If the doctor charges different rates for different visit types (initial consultation vs. follow-up), the billing staff should know the rate schedule.

### Discount Policies
- Establish a written discount policy. Common discounts: senior citizens (age 60+), clinic staff and their dependents, economically disadvantaged patients.
- Document the reason for any discount on the invoice or in the notes.
- Large or unusual discounts should require superadmin approval.

### Partial Payments
- If a patient cannot pay the full amount upfront, record the partial payment and note the balance due.
- The system tracks completed and pending payments. Follow up on outstanding balances per clinic policy.

### GST and Tax Compliance
- Ensure the clinic's GST number and tax settings are correctly configured (typically set up by the superadmin in clinic details).
- Tax applies based on the service type and local regulations. Consult with your accountant on correct tax rates.

### Invoice Integrity
- Once generated, an invoice should not be modified (except to correct genuine errors). If an invoice needs correction, void it and create a new one rather than editing the original.
- All invoices are permanently recorded and contribute to financial reports.

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | What to do |
|---|---|
| **Billing Modal won't open** | Ensure a patient is selected in the queue. The Bill button is on the Patient Header. |
| **Service items total doesn't match expected** | Check each line item's quantity and unit price. The subtotal is auto-calculated. |
| **Discount not applying correctly** | Verify whether the discount is a percentage or fixed amount. Check that it's being subtracted from the subtotal, not the grand total. |
| **Tax calculation seems off** | Tax is calculated on the discounted subtotal. Verify the tax percentage entered. |
| **Invoice number is not showing** | The invoice number is auto-generated when the bill is created. If missing, the bill may not have been saved. |
| **Print layout is cut off** | Use the browser's print dialog and ensure the page size is set to A4. Adjust margins if needed. |
| **Bill created but not appearing in financials** | Refresh the Financials page. Financial data is fetched server-side, and a newly created bill should appear. |
| **"Create Bill" button not visible** | The Bills accordion in the Administrative Footer shows a "Create Bill" button when bills exist. If no bills exist, a "Create Bill" button may appear in the empty state. Alternatively, use the Patient Header Bill button. |
