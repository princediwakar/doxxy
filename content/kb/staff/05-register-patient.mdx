## Prerequisites

- You must be logged in with a **Staff** or **Superadmin** role in the active clinic.
- You should have the patient's basic information: at minimum, their name and phone number.

## The Goal

Register a new patient in the clinic's system so they can be scheduled for appointments, have consultations documented, and be billed. A patient must exist in the system before an appointment can be booked for them.

## Step-by-Step UI Instructions

### Opening the Patient Modal

The **Patient Modal** can be opened from multiple places:

**Option A: From the Schedule page:**
1. Look for a **"New Patient"** button on the Schedule page or in the queue sidebar.

**Option B: During appointment booking:**
2. In the Appointment Modal, when selecting a patient, type a name that doesn't exist. A **"Create [name]"** option appears at the bottom of the patient search dropdown. Click it to open the Patient Modal with the name pre-filled.

**Option C: From the Command Palette:**
3. Press Cmd+K / Ctrl+K and type a name. If no match is found, a create option may appear.

### Filling in Patient Details

The **"New Patient"** modal contains the following fields:

4. **Name** (required): The patient's full name. This is the primary identifier used in search and throughout the system.

5. **Gender:** Select **Male** or **Female** using the pill/toggle buttons. This is optional but strongly recommended for clinical context.

6. **Age** (recommended): Enter the patient's age in years. This is used for demographics and clinical decision-making. Enter a number (e.g., "35").

7. **Date of Birth:** Alternative to age. If you enter the DOB, the age is calculated automatically.

8. **Phone** (strongly recommended): The patient's primary contact number. This is essential for SMS/WhatsApp appointment reminders (Clinical Excellence plan) and for contact tracing.

9. **Email** (optional): The patient's email address for digital communication.

10. **Address** (optional): The patient's residential address. Useful for demographic analysis and contact purposes.

### Saving the Patient

11. Click **"Create Patient"** (or the equivalent save button) at the bottom of the modal.

12. A green toast confirms the patient was created successfully.

13. If you opened the Patient Modal from the Appointment Modal, the new patient is automatically selected in the appointment form — you can continue booking without re-searching.

### After Registration

14. The patient now exists in the system and:
    - Appears in patient search results.
    - Can be selected when booking appointments.
    - Gets a unique **Medical Record Number (MRN)** auto-generated by the system.
    - Has an empty medical history ready for their first consultation.

15. If they walked in without an appointment, proceed to book one (see [How to Book a New Appointment](/kb/staff/booking-appointment)).

## Recommended Clinic Policy & Edge Cases

### Minimum Data Collection
- **At minimum**, collect the patient's **name** and **phone number**. Everything else can be added or updated later.
- For emergency walk-ins, name alone is sufficient — you can collect remaining details after the patient is stable.

### Phone Number Importance
- The phone number is the key to SMS/WhatsApp reminders, which significantly reduce no-show rates.
- If the patient doesn't have a phone, ask for a family member's number. Note the relationship in the Address field (e.g., "Son's phone: 9876543210").
- Verify the phone number by reading it back to the patient. A single digit error means reminders go to the wrong person.

### Age vs. Date of Birth
- If the patient knows their exact DOB, use it — it's more precise and auto-calculates age.
- If they only know their age (common for elderly patients), enter the age directly.
- For paediatric patients, record the exact DOB — age in months matters for dosing and growth charts.

### Duplicate Prevention
- Before creating a new patient, always search to check if they already exist. Common scenario: a patient registered by another staff member during a previous walk-in.
- If the patient has visited before but their name is misspelled in the system, edit the existing record rather than creating a duplicate.

### Gender Field
- The gender field currently offers Male and Female options. If a patient identifies differently, follow your clinic's policy for documentation. The field is used for demographic reporting and clinical context.
- Gender is optional but recommended — it helps with clinical decision-making, preventive care reminders, and demographic analytics.

### Address Collection
- Collect at least the locality or area — useful for demographic analysis.
- Full address is helpful for home visits if your clinic offers them.

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | What to do |
|---|---|
| **"Create Patient" button doesn't respond** | The form may have validation errors. Check that the Name field is filled. |
| **Patient created but not appearing in search** | The search index may need a moment to update. Try searching again. If still not found, the patient may have been created in a different clinic — check the active clinic. |
| **Phone number field won't accept the number** | Some validation may be applied. Ensure you're using digits only, without spaces or special characters. |
| **Patient modal closed unexpectedly** | The modal may close if you click outside it. If you lost your entered data, re-open it. The data is not auto-saved for new patient creation. |
| **"Create [name]" option not appearing in appointment modal** | The patient search dropdown shows the "Create" option only when no exact or close matches are found. Type a unique-enough name. |
