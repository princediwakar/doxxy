## Prerequisites

- You must be logged in with a **Staff** or **Superadmin** role in the active clinic.
- Medicines must have been added to inventory via procurement entries.
- A clinic must be selected.

## The Goal

Check the clinic's pharmacy inventory — see which medicines are in stock, their quantities, prices, pack sizes, and expiry dates. Identify low-stock items that need reordering and verify medicine availability before dispensing or prescribing.

## Step-by-Step UI Instructions

### From the Pharmacy page (`/pharmacy`):

The page header shows **"Pharmacy Store"** with the subtitle *"Manage medicine stock, track expiry dates, and add new purchases."* and an **"Add Stock"** button.

### Viewing Stock Levels

1. The page defaults to the **"Stock"** tab (the other tab is "Purchase Records").

2. The **Inventory Tab** displays all medicines currently in stock. Each item shows:
   - **Medicine Name:** The brand or generic name.
   - **Current Stock:** Quantity available.
   - **Expiry Date:** When this batch expires.
   - **Price:** Per-unit price.
   - **Pack Size:** How the medicine is packaged (e.g., strip of 10, bottle of 100ml).
   - **Manufacturer:** The pharmaceutical company.

3. **Low-stock items** are visually highlighted — typically with a warning colour or badge. These are medicines that have fallen below the minimum threshold and need reordering.

### Searching for a Specific Medicine

4. Use the **search bar** within the Stock tab to find a specific medicine. Type part of the name (e.g., "para", "amox", "cetirizine").

5. The list filters as you type, showing only matching medicines.

### Understanding Stock Information

6. For each medicine, note:
   - **Quantity on hand** — How many units are available for dispensing.
   - **Expiry date** — Medicines expiring soon should be used first (FEFO: First Expiry First Out).
   - **Batch number** — Important for traceability in case of recalls.
   - **Price** — The unit price used for billing when this medicine is dispensed.

### Viewing Purchase Records

7. Switch to the **"Purchase Records"** tab to see the procurement history:
   - All past stock additions (procurements).
   - Supplier details.
   - Date of purchase.
   - Medicines purchased and their quantities.
   - Total cost of each procurement.

### Adding Stock (if authorized)

8. Click **"Add Stock"** to open the Procurement Entry Sheet. See [How to Manage Pharmacy Inventory and Stock](/help/superadmin/sa-7-how-to-manage-pharmacy-inventory-and-stock) for detailed steps.

### Cross-Referencing with Prescriptions

9. When a doctor prescribes a medicine, check the Stock tab to confirm:
   - The medicine is in stock.
   - The prescribed dosage form matches what's available.
   - The quantity needed is available.

10. If the prescribed medicine is out of stock, inform the doctor so they can prescribe an alternative, or inform the patient that they need to purchase it externally.

## Recommended Clinic Policy & Edge Cases

### Daily Stock Check
- Front desk or pharmacy staff should check stock levels at the start of each day, focusing on:
  - **Fast-moving items** (common antibiotics, analgesics, antihistamines).
  - **Low-stock alerts** — items flagged by the system.
  - **Expiring items** — medicines expiring within 30 days.

### Expiry Date Management
- Always check the expiry date before dispensing. Never dispense expired medicines.
- Implement FEFO (First Expiry First Out): dispense the batch with the earliest expiry date first.
- Flag expired stock to the superadmin for removal and disposal per pharmaceutical waste guidelines.

### Stock-Out Procedure
- If a prescribed medicine is out of stock:
  1. Check if an equivalent generic or alternative brand is available.
  2. Inform the prescribing doctor if no alternative is in stock.
  3. Offer the patient a prescription to fill at an external pharmacy.
  4. Log the stock-out so the superadmin can prioritize reordering.

### Medicine Pricing for Billing
- The pharmacy price listed in inventory is used as the default when adding the medicine as a service item on a bill.
- If the purchase price changes (new procurement at a different rate), the billing price should be updated accordingly in the inventory.

### Access Control
- Both staff and superadmins can view the pharmacy. If you need to restrict who can add stock or adjust inventory, this is managed through role assignments.

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | What to do |
|---|---|
| **Stock tab shows no medicines** | No medicines have been added to inventory yet. A superadmin or authorized staff member needs to add stock via the Procurement Entry Sheet. |
| **Medicine I know exists isn't showing** | Use the search bar — the list may be long. If still not found, the medicine may be out of stock (quantity zero) or never added. Check Purchase Records. |
| **Stock quantity seems wrong** | Stock levels are updated when procurements are entered and when medicines are dispensed (via bills). If a discrepancy exists, flag it for a physical inventory count. |
| **Low-stock alert not showing for a depleted item** | The low-stock threshold may not be configured for that medicine. Report to the superadmin. |
| **Expired medicine still showing in stock** | The system does not auto-remove expired stock. Manually adjust quantities or flag for disposal. |
| **Cannot switch to Purchase Records tab** | The tabs are client-side and should switch instantly. If unresponsive, refresh the page. |
