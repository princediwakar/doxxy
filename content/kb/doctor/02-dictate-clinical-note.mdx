## Prerequisites

- You must be logged in with a **Doctor** role and have an active, non-completed appointment selected in the encounter canvas.
- Your device must have a working microphone.
- Your browser must grant microphone permission to Doxxy.
- You should be in a reasonably quiet environment for best transcription accuracy.

## The Goal

Use voice dictation during or immediately after a patient consultation to capture clinical notes hands-free. The system transcribes your speech to text in real time using Sarvam AI (with WebSocket streaming), then structures the transcript into specialty-specific SOAP fields (chief complaint, diagnosis, treatment, prescriptions, and additional findings) using an LLM structuring step.

## Step-by-Step UI Instructions

### From the Encounter Canvas (right panel on the Schedule page):

The **Dictation Zone** appears below the Patient Header and above the Inline Consultation Form. It shows a large button labelled **"Tap to Dictate"** with a microphone icon.

### Starting Dictation

1. Click **"Tap to Dictate"**. The system requests microphone permission if this is your first time.

2. Once permission is granted, the Dictation Zone transitions to the **recording state**:
   - A **timer** displays elapsed recording time (e.g., "2:35").
   - The recording area has a red-tinted border.
   - Three buttons appear: **Cancel**, **Pause**, **Done**.
   - As you speak, a live transcript buffer appears above the timer, showing your words in real time.

### During Recording

3. Speak naturally. Describe:
   - The patient's **chief complaint** and history of present illness.
   - Your **physical examination findings**.
   - Your **diagnosis** or differential diagnoses.
   - The **treatment plan** and advice.
   - Any **prescriptions** (drug name, dosage, frequency, duration).
   - Any **additional findings** or **ruled-out conditions**.

4. You can **Pause** at any time — useful if the patient asks a question or you need to perform an examination. Click **Continue** to resume recording on the same session.

5. The system performs **background lookahead structuring** during long recordings — approximately every 50 words of new speech after a 2.5-second pause. This silently pre-processes your dictation so the final structuring is faster.

### Ending Dictation

6. Click **Done** (the square stop button). The system:
   - Finalizes the transcript from the streaming session.
   - Runs the transcript through the AI structuring service (`structureTranscript`).
   - Maps the structured output to your department's specialty-specific form fields.

7. On success, a green toast appears: *"Consultation notes ready!"* with a **"View"** button that scrolls to the consultation form.

8. The consultation form auto-scrolls into view, populated with the structured data. Review and edit before completing the encounter.

### If Dictation Fails

9. If the AI structuring fails, a red toast shows the error message. The consultation form is NOT populated. You can still fill it out manually.

10. If the microphone or streaming fails, the Dictation Zone shows an error state with a **"Edit Consultation Notes"** button to open the manual form.

### Recording Additional Notes

11. After your first dictation, the Dictation Zone shows a **"Record Additional Notes"** button. Click it to capture more information.

12. Additional dictations are merged with existing structured data: new prescriptions are appended, and blank fields are filled while already-filled fields are preserved.

### Handling Connection Issues

13. If the WebSocket connection is interrupted during recording, a yellow warning banner appears: *"Connection interrupted. Audio saved locally — continue speaking."* The recording continues in **degraded mode**.

## Recommended Clinic Policy & Edge Cases

### Dictation Best Practices
- Speak clearly and at a moderate pace. The streaming STT model works best with natural, conversational speech — don't slow down artificially.
- Use structured language: "The patient reports a three-day history of fever and cough" rather than "So, um, fever, yeah, three days."
- State drug names clearly when prescribing. Spell uncommon drug names if the system misinterprets them.
- Pause between sections (complaint, exam findings, diagnosis, plan) to help the AI structure the output correctly.

### Privacy and Environment
- Dictate in a private setting. The microphone captures all ambient audio. Avoid dictating sensitive information in hallways or waiting areas.
- Background noise reduces accuracy. Close the consulting room door. If using a laptop microphone, position the device close to you.
- The transcript is processed server-side. No audio recordings are permanently stored — only the final transcript and structured output are saved to the consultation record.

### Language Support
- The Sarvam AI integration supports Indian languages. Check with your clinic administrator about configured language options.
- The system supports language selection at the start of dictation.

### When NOT to Use Dictation
- In extremely noisy environments, skip dictation and use the manual consultation form.
- For very short consultations (e.g., a quick follow-up or vaccine administration), manual entry may be faster.
- If the internet connection is unstable, use manual entry. The streaming WebSocket requires a stable connection for real-time transcription.

### Review Before Signing
- AI-generated notes are a *draft*. Always review and correct the populated form fields before completing the encounter.
- Pay special attention to drug names and dosages — STT errors in these fields can have clinical consequences.

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | What to do |
|---|---|
| **Microphone permission denied** | Your browser blocked mic access. Click the lock/site settings icon in the address bar and allow microphone access for Doxxy. Then refresh the page. |
| **"Requesting mic..." spinner shows indefinitely** | The browser is waiting for you to approve the permission prompt. Check for a permission dialog. If none appears, check your browser's site settings. |
| **Recording starts but no text appears** | Your microphone may be muted, not working, or not selected. Check your system's sound input settings. Try speaking louder. |
| **Timer runs but transcript is garbled** | Background noise, strong accent, or poor audio quality. Try moving closer to the mic, reducing ambient noise, or speaking more clearly. |
| **"Connection interrupted" warning** | Internet connectivity issue. The recording continues locally, but real-time transcription is paused. Wait for reconnection or finish and hope the final transcript is complete. |
| **"Failed to process dictation" error toast** | The structuring service failed. Your transcript was captured but could not be structured. Open the manual form and enter notes there. |
| **Structured notes are incomplete or wrong** | AI structuring is not perfect. The transcript is not displayed, but you can manually correct any fields in the consultation form. |
| **Done button spins indefinitely** | The structuring API is slow or timing out. Wait up to 30 seconds. If it persists, cancel and use manual entry. |
| **Browser doesn't support recording** | Doxxy requires a modern browser (Chrome, Edge, or Firefox) with MediaRecorder API support. Safari may have limitations. |
