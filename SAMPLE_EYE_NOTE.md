Patient is a 68-year-old female presenting with a chief complaint of progressively worsening blurry vision in the right eye over the past six months. She reports significant glare when driving at night. She denies any eye pain, redness, flashes of light, or new floaters.

On examination, uncorrected visual acuity is 20/50 in the right eye and 20/25 in the left eye. Intraocular pressure by applanation is 14 millimeters of mercury in the right eye and 15 in the left eye.

Slit lamp examination reveals normal lids and lashes bilaterally. Conjunctiva is quiet. Corneas are clear without punctate epithelial erosions. The anterior chambers are deep and quiet with no cell or flare. Examination of the lens shows two plus nuclear sclerotic cataract in the right eye, and trace nuclear sclerosis in the left eye.

Dilated fundus exam reveals a cup-to-disc ratio of 0.3 in both eyes with healthy neuroretinal rims. The maculae are flat with an intact foveal reflex. The retinal periphery is flat and attached 360 degrees bilaterally.

Assessment: Visually significant nuclear sclerotic cataract in the right eye, and a mild, non-visually significant cataract in the left eye.

Plan: Discussed the risks, benefits, and alternatives of cataract extraction. The patient wishes to proceed with surgical intervention. We will schedule her for phacoemulsification with posterior chamber intraocular lens implantation in the right eye. Order an A-scan biometry and schedule preoperative clearance.
